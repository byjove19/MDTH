Graphic Design Master Course

Master the art of visual communication with our comprehensive Graphic Design course. Learn industry-standard tools like Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign while understanding the fundamental principles of design, typography, and color theory.

This course is designed for beginners and intermediate designers who want to build a strong foundation in graphic design and create portfolio-worthy projects that will impress employers and clients alike.

What Will You Learn?

Master Adobe Creative Suite: Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign
Understand design principles, color theory, and typography
Create logos, branding materials, and marketing collateral
Design for print and digital media
Develop a professional design portfolio
Understand printing processes and file preparation
Learn to work with clients and understand design briefs
Build a professional design portfolio to showcase your skills

Requirements

  • An Open Mind & Willingness to Learn
  • A computer/Laptop (Mac or Windows)
  • Internet Connection
  • No prior design experience required

Who is this course for?

  • Beginners - People who have little to no prior knowledge of graphic design
  • Marketing Professionals - Those who want to create their own marketing materials
  • Small Business Owners - Entrepreneurs who want to design their own branding
  • Career Changers - Professionals looking to transition into graphic design

About this Course

Welcome to our Graphic design course! This comprehensive program will equip you with the skills needed to create visual content to communicate messages and ideas effectively, by combining text, images, colors, and layout techniques to produce designs that are both aesthetically pleasing and functional. Below is an overview of the curriculum.

Curriculum

Lesson 1

Visual Design

This includes the colors, typography, icons, and overall visual style of the product. The aim is to create an appealing and cohesive look for a user-friendly experience.

Lesson 2

Layout

Organizing elements on the screen in a way that is intuitive and user-friendly. This involves placing buttons, text, images, and other components effectively.

Lesson 3

Interactive Design

Designing components like buttons, sliders, and forms that users interact with. Ensuring these elements are easy to use and respond correctly to user actions.
